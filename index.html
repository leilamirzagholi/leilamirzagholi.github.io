About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at The Crowther Lab, ETH University, Zürich.

I defended my PhD in theoretical physics at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics in October 2020 under the supervision of Eiichiro Komatsu.

You can download my CV (July 2020).

Further details about me are available in an interview with the working group on equal opportunities of German Physical Society (DPG).

My partner Amir Ahmad Anvari is a postdoctoral researcher in linguistics at Institut Jean-Nicod, in Paris.

Research

Currently, my research focuses on analyzing long-term satellite images and ground observations in combination with environmental covariate datasets to quantify and forecast the effects of climate change on vegetation dynamics and biophysical properties at a global scale.
