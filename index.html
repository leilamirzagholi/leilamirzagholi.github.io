About

I am a PhD candidate in the physical cosmology group at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics (MPA) in Garching, Munich. My supervisor is Eiichiro Komatsu. My research involves the phenomenology of early Universe. Prior to this, I was a masters student at the Ludwig-Maximilians University of Munich in the elite master course in Theoretical and Mathematical Physics. I was working on modified gravity and my supervisor was Viatcheslav Mukhanov.

You can download my CV (May 2020).

Further details about me are available in an interview with the working group on equal opportunities of German Physical Society (DPG).

I am the co-organiser of the Women’s Encouragement Group (WEG) at MPA. We organise discussions with female scientists who visit Garching, and participate in the annual Girl’s Day and Open Day at MPA.

My partner Amir Ahmad Anvari is a postdoctoral researcher in linguistics at Institut Jean-Nicod, in Paris.

Research

Currently, my research focuses on building and understanding models of the early Universe with focus on phenomenological aspects (particle production and gravitational waves). To tackle the problems in these models I use a combination of analytical and numerical methods. Additionally, I am working on calculating the clustering of massive neutrinos in the vicinity of Earth using numerical simulations, these results are very important for future experiments aiming at detecting the cosmic neutrino background predicted by the big bang model.
